Disaster Preparedness
Stay Informed. Stay Safe.
Knowledge before a disaster strikes can save lives.
Emergency Kit
Prepare a bag with water, food (3-day supply), first aid, flashlight, medicines, and copies of documents.
Evacuation Plan
Know at least two evacuation routes from your home and identify a family meeting point outside your area.
Stay Connected
Keep a battery-powered or hand-crank radio for official emergency broadcasts when power is out.
Secure Your Home
Identify and fix structural vulnerabilities. Know how to shut off electricity, gas, and water quickly.
Family Communication
Designate an out-of-area contact everyone can reach. Share emergency contacts with all family members.
Medical Readiness
Store at least a 7-day supply of prescription medications. Know your nearest medical facility's location.
Flood — Do's & Don'ts

Do's

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Disconnect all electrical appliances
  • Keep emergency kit ready
  • Follow official evacuation orders
  • Help elderly and disabled neighbours

Don'ts

  • Walk through moving floodwater
  • Drive through flooded roads
  • Touch electrical equipment if wet
  • Return home before it's declared safe
  • Ignore official warnings or alerts

Disaster Type Reference
Floods
Overflow of water submerging land. Caused by heavy rain, cyclones, or dam failures. Can occur rapidly (flash floods).
Cyclones
Rotating storms with high winds and heavy rain. Rated by category (1–5). Cause storm surges and coastal flooding.
Earthquakes
Ground shaking caused by tectonic plate movement. Measured on the Richter scale. Can trigger tsunamis and landslides.
Wildfires
Uncontrolled fires in vegetation. Fueled by drought, heat, and wind. Can spread rapidly and cause significant air pollution.
